Environmentally responsible

At Lenovo, we recognize that climate change is a serious threat to the planet. We continually seek new ways to reduce harmful greenhouse gas (GHG) emissions across all of our business activities, including those of our suppliers and customers.

From everyday operations to product design and recycling solutions, we pledge to show environmental leadership across all of our activities

Thinking outside the packaging

By using bio-based materials, like bamboo and sugar cane, we are able to reduce our packaging consumption, waste, and carbon emissions levels.

End-to-end transparency

We take our responsibility as a global corporate citizen very seriously. And we demand our supply chain partners do, too.

Combatting Climate Change: Approach

Lenovo’s approach to mitigating climate change is built on a solid foundation of policy, strategy, and an ISO 14001-certified Environmental Management System (EMS). Lenovo is committed to continually reducing our global carbon footprint across all of our business activities.

Climate Change Strategy

Our strategy focuses on the following five key areas:
• Internal operations and the direct emissions from our facilities
• Energy suppliers and their operational emissions which are attributable to our activities
• Our supply chain and emissions associated with the production and delivery of goods and services
• Our customers and the emissions associated with their procurement, use, and disposal of our products
• Government, NGO, and public actions in support of transitioning to a low-carbon economy

One planet, one goal

With an unwavering commitment to a healthier planet, we’re continually striving to lessen our impact and lower our carbon footprint.


Supplier GHG Emissions

We take great effort to eliminate climate change risk in our supply chain through responsible partners and program coverage. To that end, we are proud to be members of the Responsible Business Alliance (RBA), the world’s largest industry coalition dedicated to electronics supply chain responsibility, and the CDP, a voluntary organization that aims to mitigate the dangerous impacts of climate change.
